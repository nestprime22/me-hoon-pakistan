Dr. Adeeb Rizvi (born September 1941) is a Pakistani humanitarian, renowned surgeon, and the founder of the Sindh Institute of Urology and Transplantation (SIUT). He has dedicated his life to providing free medical care to all, regardless of caste, color, or creed. Under his leadership, SIUT has become one of the largest public sector healthcare institutions in South Asia.

Early Life and Education

Dr. Rizvi was born in India and migrated to Pakistan after independence. He completed his medical education at Dow Medical College in Karachi and went on to specialize in surgery and urology in the United Kingdom. Inspired by a vision to bring change in Pakistan’s healthcare system, he returned with the mission to serve the people of his country.

Medical and Social Contributions

He started with a small urology department at Civil Hospital Karachi, which later expanded into the SIUT. His philosophy of “healthcare as a basic right” has ensured that millions of patients get treatment without discrimination or cost. He has successfully carried out thousands of kidney transplants and has established a sustainable model of free public healthcare.

Leadership and Vision

Dr. Rizvi believes that dignity in healthcare is essential. He has trained a team of skilled professionals and created a culture of empathy and service at SIUT. His ethical practices and leadership have earned him national and global recognition in the medical and humanitarian world.

Global Recognition

He has received numerous awards, including the Sitara-e-Imtiaz and Hilal-e-Imtiaz. His name is associated with ethical organ transplantation, voluntary donations, and the rights of the underprivileged. Countries around the world look to SIUT as a model of success in public health.

Legacy and Impact

Dr. Adeeb Rizvi continues to work tirelessly at SIUT, offering hope to thousands every day. His life’s work has become a symbol of compassion and service in Pakistan. The systems and values he built ensure that his legacy will continue to impact generations to come.

Famous Quotes

"We treat patients, not customers."

"Service to humanity is the best form of worship."

"No one should suffer because they cannot afford treatment."
